Key Findings
99.9%
Knitting Labor is the Real Bottleneck
Knitter capacity is fully consumed while weavers sit at 59.9% utilization. Expanding weaving capacity adds almost no value — investing in knitters directly relieves the binding constraint.
~90 units
Muffler Dominates the Optimal Mix
The MILP concentrates production heavily on Mufflers — they offer the best profit-per-knitter-day ratio in the knit product category. All other products are produced at minimum SKU levels.
α ≈ 0.75
Pareto Sweet Spot for Sustainability
At α = 0.75 profit weighting, Okhai can reduce raw material consumption by ~15% while sacrificing only ~5% of peak profit — a meaningful environmental trade-off for a craft-artisan brand.
+25% RM
Raw Material is the Highest-Leverage Input
Increasing raw material availability by 25% (combined with adding 2 knitters and 2 weavers) produces the largest profit uplift across all scenarios. Procurement negotiations are Okhai's #1 lever.
