📐 Per-Unit Economics Interpretation

Every product is profitable — margins range from 20% (Cap, Gloves) to 25% (all others). Despite lower unit profit, Muffler and Scarf dominate because they convert scarce resources most efficiently: ₹9.24/gram of raw material (tied best) and 3.50 knitter-days per unit (vs. 6.40 for Sweater). The optimizer can't simply pick the highest-margin item — it must respect that resources are scarce and non-interchangeable (woven RM ≠ knit RM, weaver-days ≠ knitter-days). Minimum SKU requirements preserve brand breadth across all 7 lines.

🎯 Pareto Message — Robustness, Not a Smooth Trade-off

Unlike textbook multi-objective problems with smooth Pareto frontiers, this model produces a binary jump: below α ≈ 0.10, the solver collapses to minimum-SKU quantities (₹62,694 profit, 7,150g RM). Above that threshold, it snaps to the full optimal solution (₹314,692, 34,430g RM). There is no smooth middle ground — the integer constraints and binding resource limits create a cliff, not a curve. This tells management that the optimal mix is robust and stable: small changes in priorities don't erode the solution. You're either operating at full capacity or you're not.

Key Findings
99.9%
Knitting Labor is the Real Bottleneck
Knitter capacity is fully consumed (389.8/390 days) while weavers sit at just 19.9% utilization. Adding weavers produces zero profit gain. Investing in knitters directly relieves the binding constraint.
Scarf 57 · Muffler 98
Two Products Drive 85% of Profit
The MILP concentrates production on Scarves (woven, ₹110K) and Mufflers (knit, ₹158K). Both achieve ₹9.24/gram — the highest raw-material efficiency. All other products are at minimum SKU levels, preserving assortment breadth.
Woven RM
Woven Raw Material is the Main Resource Lever
RHS sensitivity shows woven RM drives profit linearly from ₹245K (at 0.5×) to ₹453K (at 2.0×). Knit RM and knitter-days plateau quickly. Procurement of woven raw material is Okhai's #1 investment priority.
+19.9%
Best Scenario: RM +25% & Labour
Combining 25% more raw material with 2 additional knitters and 2 weavers lifts profit to ₹377,397 (+₹62,705). This is the maximum feasible uplift — beyond this, additional resources hit diminishing returns.
Strategic Recommendation

📋 Actionable Recommendations for Okhai Management

1. Optimize product mix now: Produce Shawl 2, Stole 5, Scarf 57, Sweater 5, Muffler 98, Cap 5, Gloves 5 for ₹314,692 total profit.
2. Negotiate woven raw material supply: This is the single highest-leverage resource. Every additional gram drives linear profit gains.
3. Hire/train knitters: Knitting labor is 99.9% consumed. Adding capacity here unlocks +8.8% profit gain. Weaver hiring adds zero value.
4. Preserve all 7 SKUs: Minimum quantities protect brand identity and store presence on the Okhai platform without materially sacrificing profit.
